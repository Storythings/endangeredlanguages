Disappearing Languages

Here is some short intro text. Max two paras. Set the scene. Language and culture are crucial to the identity of groups and communities across the world, specifically indigenous communities.

The story is of the gradual suppression and erasure of some indigenous languages since time immemorial, and what this means for the people who come from those cultures. It seeds larger questions about what that means for humanity at large, even those of us who do not right now belong to those groups.

South Italian: Italy

7,500,000 speakers remaining

Also known as Neapolitan, this language is distributed through southern Italy and has been protected by law in the region of Campania since 2008. However, the language has no official status within the rest of Italy and is not taught in schools.

Tape: Vanuatu

10 speakers remaining

Tape, also known as Maragus, is spoken only by a handful of elderly people in the coastal village of Tautu.

Highland Chontal: Mexico

2,410 speakers remaining

The speakers of this language live in the districts of Yautepec and Tehuantepec in the Mexican state of Oaxaca. It is related, but mutually unintelligible, to Lowland Chontal, which is even more endangered.
